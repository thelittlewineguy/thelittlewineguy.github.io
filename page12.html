Labor Shortages

Worker shortages in California are the result of many factors, including:
• The fact that there are not enough skilled/unskilled workers
• Changes in immigration policy by Trump administration
• Acceleration of Mexican economy
• Increased competition from Cannabis
• Workforce gets older and is not replaced
(Quackenbush, Ness, Franson) 

"60% of firms in Napa have experienced worker shortages" (Franson).

60% of firms in Napa have experienced labor shortages and 70% of firms have skipped activities due to shortages (Franson).

Worker Shortages =

• Not enough skilled and unskilled workers
• Increase in wages
• Lower efficiency (due to less experienced workers)
• Lower productivity – 70% of firms have skipped activities
• Waste
• Mechanization
