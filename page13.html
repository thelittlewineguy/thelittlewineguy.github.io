Transportation and Housing

Napa is trying to provide housing to workers, and growers are paying $10-$15 per acre to subsidize housing and the state is contributing $250,000 a year, but this only provides 180 beds and there are approximately 6,000 workers. Average rent in Sonoma County is $2,000-$2,500 a month and average farmworker makes $2,400 a month so it is difficult for farmworkers who are not in subsidized housing to “live local” (Quackenbush).

Where do you get workers?

• Sacramento/San Joaquin County and Central Valley brought in by bus by labor contractors 2-3 hours each way (Quackenbush)

• Replace with mechanization

• Increased H-2A visa pool (in 2016 134,000 H-2A visas issued nationwide; estimated 536,000 CA farmworkers) (Quackenbush)

Napa Transportation Options for workers that do not “live local”

• Bus or van by labor contractor

• Car

• Napa to Sonoma bus service ended June 30, 2017, so it now takes more than 6 hours to get from Napa to Sonoma by bus

• Government is exploring train options

Train Proposals

Proposals for public transportation include:

o Use of Napa Valley Wine train for commuter traffic between Napa and Sonoma (which opens Napa/Sonoma link, but does not help move workers in from Sacramento/ Central Valley) (Yune)

o Commuter railroad service (blue line on map) from Sacramento through Marin/Napa County (Yune)

o Ace Train or Bart Service connecting Central Valley to Bay Area (provides first link, workers still need to get from Bay Area to Napa by bus/van/car)
